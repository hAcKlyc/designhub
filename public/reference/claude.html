Can you help me analyze the key themes in this research paper about sustainable architecture? I want to understand the main arguments and how they connect.

I'd be happy to analyze the paper. Here are the three core themes I identified:

1. Biophilic Integration -- The paper argues that sustainable buildings should go beyond energy efficiency to incorporate natural systems as load-bearing design elements, not decorative afterthoughts.

2. Material Circularity -- There's a strong emphasis on closing material loops. The authors cite a case study where 92% of demolition waste was reused in new construction.

3. Adaptive Reuse Over New Build -- Perhaps the most provocative argument: the most sustainable building is one that already exists. Retrofit and adaptation yield 50-75% lower lifecycle emissions.

Transparent and honest

Claude tells you when it's uncertain, avoids making things up, and is forthcoming about its limitations. Honesty is a feature, not a constraint.
